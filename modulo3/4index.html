Tables

Simple tables

HTML Tables allow you to display and organize data in rows and collumns inside your website. It consists of an hierarchy of cells:  <th> cells and <td> cells (tableheaders and tabledata) inside a <tr> (tablerow).  HTML5 makes closing tags optional for these three tags.

Incorporating CSS you can create borders, padding, collapse borders and align the contents of a cell horizontally and vertically, besides adding colors to your table.

Bigger tables

More complex tables with more data require preciser organization, and this is what semantic table tags are for. They must be placed inside the table tag and they arrange the data in three sections:  <thead>,  <tbody> and <tfoot>.

Also, to associate header cells with data cells you can use the scope attribute. It identifies whether a <th> cell is a header for a row, column, or group of rows or columns.
